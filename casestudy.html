01 · The Problem

Billing disputes were taking too long — and no one knew why

Across the billing operations function, disputes between internal teams, finance, and partner stakeholders were a recurring friction point. Resolution cycles were lengthy, escalations were frequent, and the root causes of disputes were rarely documented or acted upon systematically.

Each dispute was treated as a one-off issue rather than a symptom of an underlying process gap. This meant the same types of errors kept recurring, consuming significant time from both the operations and finance teams and delaying revenue reconciliation cycles.

"The same billing discrepancies kept resurfacing. Without understanding why disputes were happening, we were only ever putting out fires — never preventing them."
02 · The Approach

Identifying patterns to fix the process, not just the symptoms

Rather than continuing to resolve disputes reactively, I initiated a structured root cause analysis of recurring billing issues — mapping dispute categories, frequency, and originating process steps to identify where breakdowns were consistently occurring.

01

Dispute Pattern Mapping

Compiled and categorised historical billing disputes by type, frequency, and stage of the billing cycle where they originated. This revealed that a significant proportion of disputes stemmed from the same 2–3 process gaps — data mismatches at invoice generation and unclear approval handoffs between teams.

02

Root Cause Identification

Cross-referenced dispute data with existing SOPs and workflow documentation to identify where process instructions were ambiguous or outdated. The analysis pinpointed specific handoff points where billing data was being interpreted inconsistently across teams.

03

Process & Documentation Fix

Updated the relevant SOPs and billing process documentation to eliminate ambiguity at the identified failure points. Introduced a structured dispute log template to standardise how disputes were raised and tracked — ensuring faster triage and clear ownership for resolution.

04

Stakeholder Alignment

Presented findings and updated documentation to Finance, Business Process, and Partner Management teams to drive cross-functional consensus on the revised workflow. Ensured all relevant stakeholders were aligned on the new process before rollout.

03 · The Results

Faster resolution, fewer recurrences, cleaner audits

By addressing the root causes rather than individual disputes, the frequency and resolution time of billing disputes dropped significantly — with measurable impact on both operational efficiency and audit outcomes.

✦
~40% reduction in billing dispute resolution time — achieved by eliminating recurring root causes rather than resolving disputes case by case.
✦
100% audit pass rate maintained across 4 consecutive years — supported by cleaner billing documentation and standardised dispute tracking.
✦
Reusable dispute log template adopted organisation-wide — reducing triage time and establishing clear ownership for future disputes.
✦
Cross-functional alignment across Finance, Ops, and Partner teams — reducing escalations and improving collaborative resolution of billing issues.
